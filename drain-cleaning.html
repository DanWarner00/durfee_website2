Why Drains

When to call us, not the home center auger.

A bottle of drain cleaner and a hand-snake go far. If they don't, here's when it's time.

⚠️

Multiple slow drains

If more than one fixture is slow, it's not a local clog — it's further down the line.

↩️

Backup from one fixture

Toilet backs up when the washer drains? Main-line problem, not the toilet.

🌳

Tree-root suspicion

Old Cape homes often have clay or cast-iron lines that roots invade. Camera scope confirms.

💧

Recurring clogs

Same drain clogs every 6 months? The pipe geometry or buildup needs jetting, not snaking.

👃

Smell from drains

Sulfur or sewage odor usually means a vent or trap issue. We'll find it.

🚰

Gurgling drains

Sound of air being pulled is a venting or partial-blockage tell.

FAQ

Common questions, honest answers.

How much does drain cleaning cost?+

Single-fixture snake: typically $185–$285. Main line snake: $325–$485. Hydro-jetting: $525–$895 depending on length. We quote flat-rate before we start.

Snake or jet — what's the difference?+

A snake punches through the clog. A jet scours the pipe wall clean with high-pressure water. Jetting is better for grease, sludge, and recurring clogs.

Do you do camera inspections separately?+

Yes — main-line camera scoping for home inspections or pre-purchase diligence is common.

Will hydro-jetting damage old pipes?+

On cast iron and clay, we adjust pressure carefully. We don't jet pipes that wouldn't survive it — we'll tell you straight.

Can you do same-day?+

Usually yes during business hours. Emergency main-line backups get priority.
